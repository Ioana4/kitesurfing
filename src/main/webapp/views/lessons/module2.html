Module 2 - Body Drag

Now you are ready to enter the water. Body dragging is great fun, and teaches the essential skills needed to attempt the water start and begin kiteboarding. You will be on the board sooner than you know it!

Make sure you wear a floatation vest and a wetsuit if the water is cold. Some things to consider are the depth of the water, hidden obstacles beneath of the water’s surface, strong tides and currents, and large waves.

Water re-launch

There will be times that your kite falls into the water. Remember these basic steps to re-launch easily.

1. When the kite is on the water with its leading edge facing down, swim upwind to tension the lines. Keep the bar in the normal position (red on the left)

2. Pull the bar towards you to allow the kite to pick up wind.

3. Grab one of the back leader line as far up the bar is possible and start to push the bar away. Make sure that you do not twist the line around your fingers or other body part at any time.

4. Pull on the leader line until the kite rotates on its side and starts to rise off the water. Ensure the bar is still pushed away during the take off.

5. When the kite has taken off, put both hands back on the bar to regain full control. If the kite suddenly re-launches, depower the kite by pushing the bar away.

Change direction while body dragging

You will learn the first body drag and know how to create power to pulls you. Flying the kite on one side of the wind window takes you to the same side, while flying from one side to other pulls you downwind.

Self rescue and pack-down

Self-rescue is very important skill, as it allows you to get back to the shore when things go wrong. This technique is used when the wind has died, you have a broken line or in any situation in which you cannot body drag or kiteboard back to the beach. The practice makes perfect, so try out few times in a safe environment to avoid confusion in stressful situation.

Upwind body dragging and board recovery

Do not rush!

Keep the kite parked at 45 degrees in the same direction that you were traveling.

Keep an eye on your board at all the time!

Use your body to resist the pull downwind as a keel of a boat.

Fly the kite using your upper hand and stretch out the other arm in the water, pointing it upwind (like superman!).

When you see the board over your back shoulder, it’s time to change direction.

Move your kite slowly up to 12 o’clock then over to 45 degrees in the opposite direction

Swap hands on the control bar

Body drag to opposite direction to your board.
